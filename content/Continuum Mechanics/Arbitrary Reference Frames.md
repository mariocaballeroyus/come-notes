[[Continuum Mechanics]]
## Generalization of the concept of basis

$$
\mathbf{R} = x^i \mathbf{e}_i 
\quad \quad \Rightarrow \quad \quad
\text{d} \mathbf{R} = \frac{\partial \mathbf{R}}{\partial x^i} \text{d}x^i
$$
$$
\mathbf{R} = \theta^i \mathbf{g}_i
\quad \quad \Rightarrow \quad \quad
\text{d}\mathbf{R} = \frac{\partial \mathbf{R}}{\partial \theta^i} \text{d}\theta^i
$$

$$
\mathbf{g}_i = \frac{\partial \mathbf{R}}{\partial \theta^i}
$$
## Transformation of arbitrary bases

$$
[J] = [ \frac{\partial x^i}{\partial \theta^{j'}} ]
$$
$$
[J]^{-1} = [\beta_{i}{}^{j'}]
$$
